Integrated POS means your point-of-sale software handles the customer-facing experience — menus, orders, inventory, scheduling — while CleverPays runs the payment underneath, securely and invisibly. Same workflow your staff already knows. Better payments. Better support.

How it works

From your POS to settlement, in four steps.

01

Customer pays at your POS

Cashier rings the sale on your existing POS — Clover, Lightspeed, Toast, custom — exactly the way they always have.

02

POS hands off to terminal

Your POS sends the amount to the payment terminal through middleware (FLEX, Datacap, or Eigen). Card data never touches your POS.

03

Terminal processes securely

The terminal accepts tap, dip, or swipe, encrypts the data, and submits it through CleverPays' processing infrastructure.

04

Confirmation flows back

Authorization confirmation returns to the POS, the receipt prints, and the sale closes — all in under a few seconds end-to-end.

The middleware we configure

The bridge between your POS and our payments.

Four middleware platforms, depending on what your POS and terminal need. We configure all of them — and we support them.

Semi-integrated · Most common

FLEX

The most widely-deployed semi-integrated payment solution. FLEX lets virtually any POS talk to virtually any terminal — minimal POS changes required, maximum flexibility on the hardware side.

  • Works with most major POS systems
  • Pairs with Clover, Ingenico, Pax, Verifone terminals
  • No card data on the POS — secure by design
  • Streamlined transactions, faster line speed

PIN-pad bridge · NETePay

Datacap

For integrated PIN-pad scenarios. Datacap's NETePay sits between the POS and the PIN pad (commonly Verifone P400) — encrypted, certified, and reliable. The retail-standard middleware in North America.

  • Pairs P400 PIN pad with most retail POS systems
  • TLS 1.2 encryption, full PCI compliance

For Verifone's Engage line — V400m, V400c, P400. Eigen's MiraServ middleware exposes a clean integration API and handles secure connectivity to gateways. Common in hospitality and specialty retail.

Technical advantages

Under the hood.

For the technically inclined — what semi-integrated POS actually gives you.

PCI scope reduced

Card data never enters your POS network or application. The terminal handles capture, encryption, and transmission. Your PCI compliance scope drops dramatically.

Fewer points of failure

Payments and POS are independent processes. If your POS hiccups, payments keep flowing. If a terminal needs replacing, your POS doesn't care.

Consolidated reporting

You get one reconciled view — payments side from us, sales detail from your POS — that we help you connect through your accounting or BI tool.

Common questions

Integrated POS FAQ.

What is "semi-integrated" exactly?

Semi-integrated means your POS sends the amount to the payment terminal, but never touches the card data itself. The terminal handles encryption, transmission, and confirmation. The POS sees only an authorized/declined response. This dramatically reduces your PCI scope and isolates payment failures from POS failures.

Will my staff need retraining?

Almost never. The cashier rings the sale exactly as before. The terminal handles everything else — staff just confirm the customer has completed the payment. We provide bilingual quick-reference cards and we run a walk-through with your team during go-live.

Does this affect tip prompts, gift cards, or split payments?

All three are supported and configured on a per-merchant basis. Tip prompts live on the terminal (where the customer is anyway). Gift cards integrate through the terminal or through your POS, depending on which platform owns the gift program. Split payments work natively.

What if my POS isn't on your supported list?

Ask us. The list above is just the most common — our in-house engineering team integrates with POS systems regularly, including custom-built and in-house tools. If your POS has an API or a documented integration spec, there's a strong chance we can connect to it.

Is there a setup fee?

Most standard integrations have no setup fee. Custom integrations (POS systems we haven't worked with, or unusual requirements) may have a one-time engineering charge — we tell you up-front, in writing, with a fixed scope. No surprises.

Who supports it after launch?

We do. End-to-end. Same in-house, bilingual support team that handles every other CleverPays account. If the issue turns out to be your POS vendor's responsibility, we coordinate the escalation on your behalf rather than handing you a phone number.
