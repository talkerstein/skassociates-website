Where self-service actually wins.

Self-service isn't right for every business. Where it works, it works dramatically — speed, ticket size, and staff utilization all improve at once.

QSR & counter-service restaurants

Customers order at the kiosk, staff focus on food and customer experience. Self-ordering customers also order more — average ticket lifts 15–30% in our experience.

High-traffic retail

Reduce checkout lines during peak hours. Customers buy add-ons through prompts they wouldn't see at a manned register.

Staffing-constrained operations

Restaurants and retailers struggling to staff peak hours can deploy kiosks to absorb the surge without hiring. Existing staff focus on cooking, expediting, customer service.

Kiosk FAQ

Common Kiosk questions.

Will my customers actually use a kiosk?

Most will, once trained. We help design the kiosk flow to be inviting — clear menu photos, large fonts, simple flow. Within 2–4 weeks of deployment, kiosk adoption typically reaches 40–60% of counter-service orders, with ticket sizes 15–30% higher than at the counter.

Does it work with my existing menu?

Yes. Your Clover menu syncs to the kiosk. You manage items, prices, modifiers, photos in one place — Clover Dashboard. Kiosk inherits everything.

What if a customer needs help?

The kiosk has a "Call for staff" button. Some customers always prefer counter service — we recommend keeping a Station Duo at the counter for them. Hybrid setups work best.

Can I customize the look?

Yes — through Nanonation. Your logo, brand colors, menu photography, ordering flow, upsell prompts. We configure everything during onboarding.

What about French and English?

Yes — bilingual interface, customer selects language on the welcome screen. Critical for Quebec deployments.

Accessibility?

Kiosks are wheelchair-accessible at the appropriate mounting height; we configure audio prompts and high-contrast modes as needed. AODA-compliant for Ontario merchants.
